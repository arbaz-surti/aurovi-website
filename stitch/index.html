Why I Started Aurovi

It started in my dad's factory.

I grew up watching huge machines turn out tin cans at lightning speed. But my dad never let me get caught up in the volume. He'd point at the rejects and say, "It's not about how much you make. It's about how well you make it." That stuck.

He worked eleven hours a day, six days a week. I'd wait up most nights because he'd always come home with a candy bar for me. We'd eat dinner late, together, then he'd be gone again before I woke up.

His business never grew the way it could have. Not because he wasn't great at what he did, but because he never had the time. Every hour got eaten by paperwork, managing workers, legal stuff, the daily grind of keeping things running. He had one client he served beautifully for years. He always wished he could've served more.

I spent the next decade in tech, across QA, business analysis, and product management. Different roles, same pattern: brilliant people drowning in work that didn't actually need a human touch. I became obsessed with customer empathy, with understanding exactly what made people's days harder so I could build them out of the trap.

Then I started learning about AI automation. I remember watching a workflow that took an incoming lead, dropped it into a CRM, and triggered an entire email sequence with zero human intervention. My jaw dropped. The kind of work that ate my dad's evenings? AI can handle most of it now.

That's why Aurovi exists. To give small business owners back the hours they've been trading away just to keep the lights on, so they can finally do what my dad always wanted to do: grow.
